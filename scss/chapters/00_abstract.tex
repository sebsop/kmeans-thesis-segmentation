Real-time 5D image and video segmentation is a fundamental preprocessing step in edge computer vision, but standard CPUs cannot execute multi-dimensional clustering within strict real-time limits (120+ FPS). This paper introduces a GPU-accelerated C++/CUDA framework for 5D segmentation on a joint color-spatial manifold. The system compares a classical Euclidean distance solver against a quantum-inspired engine emulating the quantum SWAP test. CUDA kernel performance is maximized through optimized thread layouts and memory access patterns, while an asynchronous thread handles diagnostics. Tested on an NVIDIA GeForce RTX 5070 Ti, the pipeline achieves throughputs up to 750 FPS (259 FPS under the heaviest K=40, S=1 configuration). Due to the monotonic nature of the phase-space mapping, the quantum-inspired engine is mathematically and visually equivalent to classical K-Means, maintaining speed parity and identical cluster boundaries under varying lighting conditions.
